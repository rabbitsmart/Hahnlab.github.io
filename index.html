Human and Robot Interaction

We study human-robot interaction inthe perspective of cognitive psychology. We use state-of-the-art technologies like robotics, virtual reality, and machine learning. We aim to create a valuable and practical knowledge to help society to live with technology.

Virtual Reality

Virtual reality is the safest (and fun!) way to look at human behavior and cognitive processing such as crossing a simulated road.

Conversational Agents

With the advancement of natural language processing technology, we can look into what makes a trustworthy and equitable conversational agent.

Pedestrians & Autonomous Vehicles

We will soon live in a smart city where every location we visit will be shared with other drivers and even see a car that has no driver. We aim to study what makes a safe interaction between pedestrians and self-driving cars along withboth individual and cultural differences intent communication.

  About Us

From self-driving cars to healthcare tools, robotics and artificial intelligence are becoming increasingly prevalent in our everyday lives. This is in fact only a small part of tsunami of 4th industrial revolution technologies. Consequently, understanding human cognitive function and social intelligence among such technologies is important now more than ever. In our lab, we investigate many aspects of human cognition and emotion in order to promote better systems designs and more fluid human-robot interaction. We ultimately aim to contribute to future society by providing knowledges of efficient future-tech applications in real life with minimal negative side effects. Some questions we ask include: What are key factors that influence people to give higher trust to an automated system? What are ethical concerns for designing a robot? How does one develop computational models of social intelligence to allow for natural human-robot interaction? What role does our emotion play in an automated environment? Specifically, we take human experimentation methods and rigorous data analyses to investigate how people perceive, understand, and utilize robots and automated systems.

Human Robot Interaction (HRI)
With robots becoming an essential part of our lives, it is becoming evermore important to understand how humans psychologically deal with such intelligence. Especially the advent of "Social Robots" presents more emphasis on the matter.With research on the topic of HRI we aim to investigate efficient ways to implement robot technologies to our daily lives.
Emotion and Cognition
In recent years our attention to human emotion has grown and it became an important factor when desgning products and services. In our lab, we investigate different aspects of emotion and cognition, such as the influence of emotion on attention, affective evaluation, visual and auditory processing, etc.
Aging and Interface Design
The graying of populations is a general trend across the globe. As it is speculated that by 2050, majority of our society are to be older than 50, human-technology interface clearly should incorporate the needs of older users. In our lab, our research focuses on physical, perceptual, and cognitive aspects of aging that is relevent to interface design.
Emotional Design
User experience(UX) is a concept that is gaining more importance every year. It is no coincidence that every major manufacturing compaines have their own seperate UX design team to make their products more user friendly. As Emotion plays a significant role in interaction with products, it is essential to thoroughly investigate the psychological details of this interaction.
